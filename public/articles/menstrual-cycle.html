Understanding Your Menstrual Cycle

Last Updated: March 15, 2024 10 min read

What is the Menstrual Cycle?

The menstrual cycle is a natural process that occurs in women's bodies, typically lasting between 21 to 35 days. It's controlled by hormones and prepares the body for potential pregnancy each month.

Phases of the Menstrual Cycle

Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)

  • Shedding of uterine lining
  • Bleeding occurs
  • Hormone levels are low

Follicular Phase (Days 6-14)

  • Egg follicles develop
  • Estrogen levels rise
  • Uterine lining thickens

Ovulation (Day 14)

  • Egg is released
  • Fertile window begins
  • LH hormone peaks

Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)

  • Progesterone levels rise
  • Uterine lining prepares
  • PMS symptoms may occur

Common Symptoms and What They Mean

Symptom Possible Cause When to Seek Help
Cramps Uterine contractions Severe pain, lasts >3 days
Mood Changes Hormone fluctuations Severe depression/anxiety
Breast Tenderness Hormone changes Severe pain, lumps

When to See a Doctor

  • Irregular periods for more than 3 months
  • Severe pain that affects daily activities
  • Heavy bleeding (soaking through a pad/tampon every hour)
  • Periods lasting longer than 7 days
